How to Play: Modules, Meta Upgrades, and Strategy

Because this game is RNG-free, planning beats luck: the same decisions produce the same outcomes. Use the sections below to understand how modules shape your build, how meta upgrades scale your progress, and how to iterate on strategy.

1) Core Loop (How a Run Works)

Your goal each run is to progress consistently through fixed-length waves and keep your reward multiplier healthy.

  • Place towers on the neon grid; aim for stability early.
  • Each wave ends on a fixed timer; your performance is tracked via Kill Ratio and a required Threshold.
  • If you fall below the threshold, your reward multiplier drops‚Äîbalance your build to prevent that.
  • Progress is consistent and repeatable: the same decisions produce the same outcomes.

2) Modules (Your Build‚Äôs Backbone)

Modules shape your tower stats, economy, and survivability‚Äîthink of them as role-defining parts of your build.

  • More slots unlock more synergy‚Äîchoosing which slots to unlock first is a real strategic decision.
  • Balance OFFENSE / DEFENSE / UTILITY; over-leaning into one axis can make thresholds fragile.
  • Module upgrades may look small, but in a deterministic system their compounded impact matters.
  • Keep experiments repeatable: the same module set yields the same baseline to optimize against.

3) Meta Upgrades (Permanent Power)

Spend points earned from runs on permanent upgrades to raise your baseline for future runs.

  • Offense: permanent boosts to damage, fire rate, crit effectiveness, and similar metrics.
  • Defense: base durability, escape-related mitigation, or sustain-style bonuses.
  • Economy: small but critical edges like gold multipliers or cost curve relief.
  • Invest with a goal: do you want a faster start, or a higher ceiling?

4) Building Strategy (Practical Guide)

Determinism lets you improve like a lab experiment: measure, compare, iterate.

  • Early game: secure your kill ratio; prioritize consistency over greedy spikes.
  • Mid game: complete module synergies; maintain a defense/economy balance.
  • Late game: identify one bottleneck (DPS, range, durability) and focus upgrades there.
  • Change one variable at a time so comparisons stay meaningful.
